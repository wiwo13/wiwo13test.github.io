Music Genres

  • Indie
    • "Indie" is short for "independent," and in the context of music and entertainment, it refers to works created and produced independently, without the involvement or financial support of major record labels, studios, or corporations. Indie music, films, video games, and other forms of media are typically created by individuals or small, often grassroots, organizations.
    See Artists
  • Rock
    • Rock music is a genre of popular music that originated as "rock and roll" in the United States in the late 1940s and early 1950s. It has since evolved into a diverse and influential genre known for its distinctive sound characterized by the use of electric guitars, bass guitars, drums, and powerful vocals.
    See Artists
  • Hip hop music
    • Hip-hop music is characterized by its rhythmic and lyrical style, often featuring spoken-word poetry, rap, and rhyming lyrics delivered over a beat. It typically incorporates elements of sampling, DJ scratching, and various musical styles, including funk, soul, jazz, and electronic music.
    See Artists
  • K-pop
    • K-pop, short for Korean pop music, is a popular music genre and cultural phenomenon that originated in South Korea. It is characterized by a fusion of various musical styles, including pop, hip-hop, R&B, and electronic dance music, often accompanied by visually striking music videos and choreographed dance routines.
    See Artists
  • Country music
    • Country music is a genre of music that features a variety of musical styles, including folk, blues, and gospel, often characterized by its emphasis on storytelling through lyrics and the use of instruments such as acoustic guitars, fiddles, steel guitars, and harmonicas. It often explores themes of love, heartbreak, rural life, and everyday experiences.
    See Artists
  • Alternative Rock
    • Alternative rock, often abbreviated as alt-rock, is a diverse and influential music genre that emerged in the 1980s and gained mainstream popularity in the 1990s. It is characterized by its departure from the conventional sound of mainstream rock music and its willingness to experiment with various musical styles and themes.
